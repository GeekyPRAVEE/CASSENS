Cassens Transport Company

Cassens Transport Company has served the automotive industry proudly since 1933. Today, we employ more than 1,400 Teamster drivers, 150 shop personnel, and over 250 other support people.

Each day our modern fleet delivers up to 10,000 vehicles for 15 domestic and international automobile manufacturers. Our distinctive red equipment can be seen throughout United States and Ontario, concentrating our services in the Midwest, Atlantic seaboard and Ontario.

Our company headquarters is located in Edwardsville, Illinois, 25 miles northeast of St. Louis, Missouri.

Modern Technology

We have the most modern fleet in our industry. Our advanced tractor and trailer technology enables us to maximize our loads and provide ease of loading to meet our customer requirements and protect our drivers and the public.


Committed to Safety

Our company believes that safety and health in the workplace are of paramount importance. It is our objective to provide you a safe working environment and regular training in the areas of injury prevention, accidents, and OSHA awareness. Ultimately, responsibility for working and acting safely rests with you the driver and we do everything that we can to aid our drivers in this endeavour. We have high expectations of you, and we do everything we can to prepare you for the job.


Outstanding Pay / Benefits

Cassens drivers receive full coverage Teamster health, welfare and pension benefits. We pay according to the Teamster’s National Master Automobile Transporters Agreement.


Time with Your Family

Our average trip is 300 miles. Drivers are usually away from home only one or two days at a time, which allows time to spend with your family. Our terminals offer a variety of assignments based upon seniority. At many locations you will have the opportunity to bid on different assignments to better accommodate your family situation.


Training and Orientation

Each new Cassens driver will receive hands-on training from our staff on the proper methods we expect to be used when handling, loading and unloading vehicles. New hires will also receive training in Safety and Wellness, Smith System, and Daily Log Entry.


Mentor Program

All newly hired drivers are matched with an existing employee who will serve as a mentor. The mentor will work with the new employee and help them become a member of the Cassens “family.”


Maintaining a Tradition of Excellence

Cassens Transport Company has a strong tradition of quality service and innovation. We have received numerous awards recognizing our tradition of excellence from our customers. Our drivers are skilled, professional, courteous, and hard working. We expect the same from you.


What We Need to Know About You

We need reliable employees to help us continue our strong tradition as an industry leader. We offer solid career opportunities for good people who meet the following qualifications.


Driver Qualifications

  1. You must posses a valid Class “A” CDL (Commercial Driver’s License) valid for interstate transportation issued only by one state or jurisdiction
  2. You must be physically qualified to drive a commercial motor vehicle.(DOT Federal Highway Administration’s Safety Regulations Subpart B, Section 391.41)
  3. You must have one year of truck driving experience with tractor-trailer equipment or be a graduate of an accredited truck driving school.
  4. You must have a high school diploma or GED.
  5. You must not be currently disqualified from operating a commercial motor vehicle.
  6. You must be able to drive a manual transmission.
  7. You must be able to read and speak the English language sufficiently to converse with the general public, to understand highway traffic signs and signals in the English language, to respond to official inquiries, and to make entries on reports and records.
  8. You will be disqualified from employment if you have any record of having a driver’s license suspension or revocation lasting for more than 30 days which resulted directly from a conviction while operating a motor vehicle within the 36-month period immediately prior to the company’s receipt of your employment application.
